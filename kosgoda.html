Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

About Kosgoda Hatchery

Kosgoda Hatchery, also known as the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project, is a renowned turtle conservation initiative located in the town of Kosgoda, Sri Lanka. Established in 1988, the hatchery aims to protect sea turtles from threats such as habitat destruction, poaching, and accidental entanglement in fishing nets.

Conservation Efforts

The main focus of the Kosgoda Hatchery is the conservation and rehabilitation of several species of sea turtles, including the Green turtle, Hawksbill turtle, Loggerhead turtle, Olive Ridley turtle, and Leatherback turtle. The hatchery collects and protects sea turtle eggs, provides a safe environment for hatchlings, and conducts educational programs and awareness campaigns to promote sea turtle conservation.
